## 🔑 The Core of This Topic
Overthinking, scope creep, and structural diffing are common pitfalls that can sabotage even the best-planned projects, leading to delays, increased costs, and reduced quality.

## ⚡ 5-Second Key Points
- **Overthinking**: leads to analysis paralysis
- **Scope Creep**: adds unnecessary features and complexity
- **Structural Diffing**: causes unnecessary rework and delays

## 📈 Detailed Breakdown
**Overthinking**
Overthinking can cause indecision and a lack of progress, as individuals become mired in details and what-ifs. 
**Scope Creep**
Scope creep occurs when new features or requirements are added to a project without proper planning or consideration of the impact on the overall project.

> 💡 Insight: setting clear goals and boundaries is essential to avoiding these pitfalls

## 🎯 Real-World Impact
- Delays and cost overruns
- Reduced quality and increased bugs
- Decreased team morale and motivation

## ✨ Conclusion
By being aware of these common pitfalls and taking steps to avoid them, individuals and teams can ensure their projects are completed on time, within budget, and to the desired quality.
