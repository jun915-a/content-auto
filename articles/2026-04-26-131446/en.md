# West Forgets Coding

The West is losing its manufacturing edge and now its coding skills, what's next?

## 🔑 The Core of This Topic
The West's decline in manufacturing has been followed by a decline in coding skills, a trend that could have far-reaching consequences for the economy and society.

## ⚡ 5-Second Key Points
- **Loss of Manufacturing**: The West has lost its manufacturing edge to countries like China and India.
- **Coding Skills Decline**: The West is now experiencing a decline in coding skills, with fewer students pursuing careers in tech.
- **Economic Consequences**: This decline could have significant economic consequences, including lost jobs and reduced competitiveness.

## 📈 Detailed Breakdown
**Element 1**
The decline in coding skills is attributed to a lack of interest in tech careers among young people, as well as a lack of investment in tech education.

**Element 2**
The consequences of this decline are already being felt, with many companies struggling to find qualified coders and tech professionals.

> 💡 Insight: The West needs to invest in tech education and make coding a more attractive career path to remain competitive.

## 🎯 Real-World Impact
- Lost jobs and reduced economic growth
- Reduced competitiveness in the global market
- Decreased innovation and technological advancements

## ✨ Conclusion
The West's decline in coding skills is a serious issue that needs to be addressed through investment in tech education and making coding a more attractive career path.
