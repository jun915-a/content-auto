# Apple Patches iPhone Bug Used by Cops to Retrieve Deleted Messages

TechCrunch has learned that Apple has quietly patched a vulnerability in its iPhones that allowed law enforcement to extract deleted messages from devices. This exploit was reportedly used by police in the United States to gather evidence in investigations.

## 📈 Detailed Breakdown
**The Exploit**: The security bug was discovered by a security researcher who found that Apple's iPhone operating system was vulnerable to an exploit that could extract deleted messages from devices. The researcher discovered that the exploit worked by exploiting a weakness in Apple's secure erase feature, which is designed to wipe all data from a device when it is erased.

**How It Was Used**: The exploit was reportedly used by police in the United States to gather evidence in investigations. Police would obtain a search warrant for a suspect's iPhone and then use the exploit to extract deleted messages from the device. The extracted messages would then be used as evidence in court.
