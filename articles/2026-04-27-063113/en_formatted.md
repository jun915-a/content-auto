# Rethinking SWE-bench

*Insert header image here*

Discover why SWE-bench Verified no longer measures frontier coding capabilities and what this means for the future of coding assessments

## 🔑 The Core of This Topic
SWE-bench Verified was once the gold standard for evaluating coding skills, but it no longer measures frontier coding capabilities due to its limitations in assessing complex problem-solving skills.
## ⚡ 5-Second Key Points
- **Point 1**: SWE-bench focuses on basic coding skills
- **Point 2**: It lacks real-world application assessments
- **Point 3**: New methods are needed to evaluate coder capabilities
## 📈 Detailed Breakdown
**Element 1**: The SWE-bench Verified exam primarily tests fundamental coding concepts, which are essential but not sufficient for complex software development.
**Element 2**: Real-world coding involves more than just writing code, it requires understanding the context, working with teams, and adapting to new technologies.
> 💡 Insight: The future of coding assessments must include evaluations of complex problem-solving, collaboration, and adaptability.
## 🎯 Real-World Impact
- More accurate assessments of coding skills are needed to ensure the best candidates are selected for jobs
- New evaluation methods can help reduce bias in hiring processes
- The shift away from SWE-bench Verified encourages innovation in coding assessments
## ✨ Conclusion
The end of SWE-bench Verified as a measure of frontier coding capabilities marks the beginning of a new era in coding assessments, one that prioritizes real-world skills and complex problem-solving.
